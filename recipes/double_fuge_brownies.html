Double Fudge Brownies

Double chocolate brownies from scratch recipe that are easy, tasty, and the absolute best way to cure your chocolate craving. We used Ghiradelli chocolate chips and Ghiradelli semi-sweet chocolate bars for this one. You can make these brownies with frosting if you'd like for more of a cake experience.

I was craving a really chocolatey, ooey, gooey, chocolate chip filled double chocolate brownie today, and boy, am I glad I made this recipe. It definitely fit the bill.

The texture of the brownie inside is chewy and fudgy, the top has the perfect crackle and the insides are filled with melting chocolate chips. Simply decadent.

  • Salted Butter
  • Two Semi-Sweet 4 oz chocolate bars (I used Ghiradelli), chopped
  • Granulated Sugar
  • Light or Dark Brown Sugar
  • 3 Eggs
  • Vanilla Extract
  • All-purpose Flour
  • Cocoa Powder
  • Salt
  • Semi-Sweet Chocolate Chips

I really, really love the crackly tops on these brownies. Perfection. This time, I used Ghiradelli chocolate disks instead of chips and they worked really well.

How to make Double Chocolate Brownies?

  1. Melt chocolate and butter in a saucepan over medium heat until smooth and melted. Remove from heat and cool in a mixing bowl for 10 minutes.
  2. Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Line an 8x8 baking dish with parchment paper.
  3. Whisk in granulated and brown sugars to chocolate/butter mixture. Add eggs, one at a time, whisking well until smooth. Whisk in vanilla.
  4. Add in flour, cocoa powder, and salt, slowly, just until combined.
  5. Fold in chocolate chips.
  6. Cook for 34-37 minutes in the preheated oven.

How do you know when fudge brownies are done?

When you insert a toothpick in the center of the brownies and there are a few moist crumbs on the toothpick, your brownies are done. If there is still wet dough clinging to the toothpick, the brownies need to cook longer (at least a few more minutes).
